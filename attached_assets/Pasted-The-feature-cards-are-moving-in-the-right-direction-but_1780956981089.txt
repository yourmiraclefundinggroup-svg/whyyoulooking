The feature cards are moving in the right direction but the screenshots currently feel like raw screenshots dropped into containers.

I want these treated like product photography.

For every feature card:

UNDERSTAND
PRIORITIZE
ACT
TRACK

Use the following design rules:

1. Consistent screenshot framing

All screenshots should occupy roughly the same visual space.

The visible UI should begin at approximately the same vertical position.

No screenshot should appear significantly zoomed in or zoomed out compared to the others.

2. Remove empty space

Crop out unnecessary whitespace and margins from screenshots.

Show only the most important part of the interface.

Focus on the content that supports the card headline.

3. Create visual focal points

Understand:
Show findings, insights, highlighted issues.

Prioritize:
Show recommendations, action plan, ranking.

Act:
Show tools being used, dispute workflow, action center.

Track:
Show score movement, progress, milestones.

The most important information should sit near the center of the crop.

4. Apply Apple-style presentation

Screenshots should feel like premium product renders.

Use:

- 16px–24px radius
- subtle shadow
- soft edge fade
- slight zoom
- careful cropping

Do not show entire raw screens.

Frame each screenshot intentionally.

5. Equal card composition

Every card should follow:

[Visual Area]
approximately 65–70% height

[Headline + Copy]
approximately 30–35% height

The screenshot area should be consistent across all four cards.

6. Add depth

Allow screenshots to slightly overflow upward within the card.

Create the feeling that the product is emerging from the surface.

Use masking and gradient fades if necessary.

7. Match premium SaaS examples

Reference:

- Apple product pages
- Linear feature cards
- Arc Browser website
- Stripe product showcases

Goal:

The cards should feel like carefully art-directed product showcases, not screenshots placed inside boxes.
