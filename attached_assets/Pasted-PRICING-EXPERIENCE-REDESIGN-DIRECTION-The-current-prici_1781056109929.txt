PRICING EXPERIENCE REDESIGN DIRECTION

The current pricing implementation is technically correct, but it feels like a traditional pricing page.

That is NOT the design direction.

The ScoreShift pricing experience should feel like an extension of the landing page, not a utility page attached to it.

The landing page currently feels premium, spacious, cinematic, and intentional. The pricing experience should maintain that same feeling from beginning to end.

DO NOT build pricing as a collection of small sections stacked on a page.

DO NOT make pricing feel like a SaaS checkout page.

DO NOT compress sections vertically.

DO NOT make the pricing cards the entire experience.

Instead:

Treat each stage of the pricing journey as a full-screen experience similar to the landing page.

The user should feel like they are moving through a guided story.

The structure should be:

01 — Choose Your Path
Full-screen hero section using the Split Path image.
Large typography.
Glass cards.
Plenty of breathing room.

02A — Self-Service Hero
Full-screen section using the Winding Path image.

03A — Self-Service Pricing
Full-screen section using the Cream Environment image as the background.
Pricing cards float within the environment.

04A — Self-Service Comparison
Full-width premium comparison section.

05A — ScoreShift Mail Wallet
Full-screen premium section.
Not a small component.

06A — Self-Service CTA
Large cinematic closing section.

The Concierge experience follows the same structure:

02B — Concierge Hero
Full-screen hero using the Concierge image.

03B — Concierge Pricing
Full-screen pricing environment.

04B — Concierge Comparison

05B — ScoreShift Mail Wallet

06B — Concierge CTA

Every major section should feel like a landing-page scene.

The user should never feel like they have left the ScoreShift world.

Spacing should be generous.

Typography should be large.

Images should have room to breathe.

Cards should float inside environments rather than sit inside generic containers.

The visual goal is:

Apple
Linear
Luxury Hospitality
Private Banking

NOT:

Traditional SaaS pricing pages
Credit repair websites
Checkout pages
Comparison websites

The pricing experience should feel like a premium guided journey that helps the user confidently choose the path that fits them best.
