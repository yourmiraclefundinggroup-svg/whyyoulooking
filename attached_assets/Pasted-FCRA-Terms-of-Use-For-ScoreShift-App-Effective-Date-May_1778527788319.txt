Fair Credit Reporting Act (“FCRA”) Rights

The Fair Credit Reporting Act is a federal law that promotes the accuracy, fairness, and privacy of information contained in consumer credit reports.

Under the FCRA, consumers have important rights, including:

The right to be informed if information in your file has been used against you.
The right to know what is in your credit file.
The right to request and obtain your credit score.
The right to dispute incomplete or inaccurate information.
The right to place fraud alerts and security freezes on your credit file.
The right to seek damages from violators of the FCRA.
The right to limit certain prescreened credit offers.

Additional information regarding your FCRA rights can be found at the official website of the Federal Trade Commission
 and the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
.
